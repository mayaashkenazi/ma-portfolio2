Improving Design by Iteration

Marathon Education

Wireframing • Prototyping • Figma • User Testing

As part of a group project in my senior UIUX class ↗, I mocked up a solution to an emerging startup's concept, desgined an interactive interface, implemented critique feedback, and conducted user testing.

Role: UIUX Student

Duration: October - November 2022

The Question

How can we best design a solution for Marathon Education ↗'s mission of providing live after-school tutoring and classes to K-12 students in Vietnam?

How can we best design a solution for Marathon Education ↗'s mission of providing live after-school tutoring and classes to K-12 students in Vietnam?

The Process

1

Sketching

We first spent a few minutes separately sketching, and then we shared our initial visions.

2

Low-Fidelity Wireframing

We then combined our ideas to produce an initial, low-fidelity wireframe. I provided the visual design for the onboarding process, and two of my other teammates provided visual designs for the home page and live class, respectively. I was also responsible for translating our ideas and building our flows on the Figma document.

3

High-Fidelity Prototyping

Once we completed our wireframe, we attended a critique session, where we presented our work and received helpful feedback. Here are a few critiques that were especially helpful:
  • Include description of company on first sign up page
  • Make recommended courses more relevant/informed by user sign up
  • Include username/password section of sign up
  • Simplify the home page
  • Edit "slow down" and "speed up" options to make more clear if classes are recorded or live
  • Make search results clickable in their entirety
Using the feedback we received in the studio critique, we created our high-fidelity prototypes.

4

User Testing

We then conducted usability tests of our high-fidelity prototype on UserTesting.com ↗. Here, I was responsible for writing up testing materials, including instructions, tasks, and post-task questions.

Imagine you’re a 6th grade student, seeking help in your Algebra studies. Sign up for Marathon Education and register for the 6th grade Algebra class. Once you’ve registered for this class, join the live session. Please think aloud as you are performing the tasks.

You will be interacting with an interactive mockup, not an actual website. Not all buttons will work, but imagine that they would be functional in the final prototype. If at any point you are unsure of how to continue, press anywhere on the screen to see the available next steps highlighted in blue.

  • Follow the first few pages to sign up for Marathon Education. You will know that you have completed this task successfully when you have reached the home page.
  • Register for the 6th grade Algebra class. You will know that you have completed this task successfully when the 6th grade Algebra class appears in the ‘My Classes’ tab.
  • Join the live session of the 6th grade Algebra class. You will know that you have completed this task successfully when you see a screen with math equations and student questions.

What frustrated you most about this app?

  1. The colors, and design
  2. No frustration about the app
  3. The only thing that frustrated me was that 6th grade Algebra wasn't listed in the menu of choices

What did you like about this app?

  1. It was really easy to use, but it has an old vibe
  2. The simplicity made it easy to maneuver, and the screen is not too busy
  3. I liked that it was easy to register, sign up and start a class

Do you think this app is accessible for a wide range of ages? What is the youngest age this app is appropriate for?

  1. Yes! I think like 8 would be the youngest age
  2. 6th grade is a little on the young side, in my opinion. Due to kids getting distracted and needing to select the correct class
  3. I think that 6th grade and up would be the best choice for this app. And I think anyone older than that could easily use it. The youngest age I would think could use it well would be about 11 year olds

Any other comments?

  1. No
  2. No other comments about the app
  3. I admire you for trying have tools to teach math online. I think it would be pretty hard to do
All users who participated in our usability tests were able to complete all tasks successfully and with ease. The most critical piece of feedback we received was on our visual style; specifically, one user felt the visual design used outdated features (tabs) and outdated styles (the dark blue color and font). Based on this response, we would opt for more modern designs and structures (for example, by switching the tabs for a bottom menu).

All users who participated in our usability tests were able to complete all tasks successfully and with ease. The most critical piece of feedback we received was on our visual style; specifically, one user felt the visual design used outdated features (tabs) and outdated styles (the dark blue color and font). Based on this response, we would opt for more modern designs and structures (for example, by switching the tabs for a bottom menu).

The Solution

At each point in our iterative design process, we gained insights that led us closer to what the final product should look like. After critique, we shifted our focus to simplifying and clarifying the structure of our solution. If we were to continue after our usability tests, we would shift our focus once more, this time to designing more engaging and modern visuals.

The Takeaways

During this project, I learned to design as part of a group. More specifically, I learned how to combine multiple, sometimes contradicting, visions of a product to produce a single, cohesive solution. Further, I learned the benefits of an iterative design process. By exposing a product to a variety of different potential users and by continuously testing and tweaking it, a designer approaches a more usable and exciting solution.
