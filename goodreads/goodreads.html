Redesigning for Accessibility & Usability

GoodReads

Wireframing • Prototyping • Figma

I redesigned an alternative to the GoodReads Listopia page ↗, to improve usability and accessibility.

Role: UIUX Student

Duration: September - October 2022

The Question

What are the current accessibility and usability issues with the GoodReads Listopia page ↗? How can it be redesigned to address these issues?

What are the current accessibility and usability issues with the GoodReads Listopia page ↗? How can it be redesigned to address these issues?

The Process

1

Assessing Issues

First, I assessed the key usability and accessibility issues.
Usability Issues

New users may be confused about:

  • Why the list categories aren't all in the same location and format (compare featured/recent activity in center with best of/brand new on right)
  • Why there are two search bars (one for general and one for tags)
  • Why they can't browse all of their lists at the same time

Casual users may forget

  • Where different book list categories are
  • Where and how to search their lists
  • How to browse

Frequent users may be slowed down by:

  • "All Lists" link that leads to main Listopia page (e.g. goes nowhere)
  • Irrelevant lists with little traction in recent activity and brand new list categories
  • The inefficient browsing process
Accessibility Issues

  • WebAIM WAVE ↗ reveals that the page has 60 instances of low contrast (number of books and voters written in very light text on white background), which makes some text unreadable for those who are visually impaired

  • Small text makes the page difficult to read for those who are visually impaired
  • Small spaces between links (e.g. lists I've created/voted on/liked) makes selecting options difficult for those with impairments relating to fine motor skills

  • Page is not adaptable to different screen sizes, so is inaccessible to those viewing on non-desktop devices or tab sizes

Once I was satisfied with my low-fidelity wireframes, I created a visual design style guide. I attempted to retain as much of the original design as possible, while introducing new elements and interactions that would make the page more usable and accessible.

The Solution

My redesign of the GoodReads Listopia page improves upon usability by unifying the list category display, eliminating the second unnecessary search bar, and simplifying the browsing process. Further, it addresses accessibility issues by increasing text contrast, making text and spaces between links larger, and accommodating various screen sizes.

The Takeaways

This project made me more aware of common unusable, inaccessible web habits and potential solutions to them. It encouraged me to think more critically about different users' abilities and strategies to ease potential interface pain points.
