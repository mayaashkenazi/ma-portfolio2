Representing User Behavior

CVS Pharmacy

Sketching • User Interviews • Personas • Storyboarding

For an assignment in my senior year UIUX class ↗, I observed and interviewed users interacting with CVS checkout machines, created personas, and illustrated a storyboard.

Role: UIUX Student

Duration: September - October 2022

The Question

Why are CVS machine checkout stations such enticing alternatives to human checkout stations? How can these checkout stations be further improved?

Why are CVS machine checkout stations such enticing alternatives to human checkout stations? How can these checkout stations be further improved?

The Process

1

Sketches

First, I sketched a checkout machine flow in Balsamiq to familiarize myself with the interface.

2

Observations & Interviews

I then observed and interviewed checkout machine users.

  • Customers stand in line for machine checkouts even when human checkouts available
  • Various machine checkout issues requiring human cashier assistance (e.g. age restricted items)
  • Compared to human chechouts, machine checkouts are used for quicker purchases of fewer items

  • Why did you choose to come to this convenience store today instead of others?
  • Why did you use the machine checkout station instead of the human one?
  • What is your favorite thing about this station?
  • What is your least favorite thing about this station?
  • What would you change about this station?

From my interviews, I learned that customers:

  • Choose this convenience store because it is closest to where they live (many are students)
  • Prefer machine checkout because they believe it's faster
  • Like the minimal interaction with other humans (especially when buying sensitive items)
  • Dislike when human assistance/intervention necessary

3

Personas

Next, I used my observations and interview responses to generate 2 personas.

4

Storyboard

I then produced a storyboard for the user from my first persona.

The Solution

Since users choose machine checkouts over human checkouts for speed and privacy reasons, they get frustrated when human assistance is necessary. CVS would benefit from minimizing such interactions, perhaps by allowing CVS employees to access and override machine checkout alerts on their handheld devices instead of only at the stations.

The Takeaways

While working on this project, I learned how to merge insights from observations and interview responses into personas and a storyboard which reflect explicit users' experiences, highlighting their goals and frustrations. This project helped make me a more attentive and empathetic product designer.
