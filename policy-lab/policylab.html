Optimizing COVID-19 Testing

Brown University

User Interviews • Data Analysis • NVivo

With The Policy Lab ↗ at Brown University, I was involved in a UX research project to optimize K-12 COVID-19 testing in Rhode Island. I conducted interviews and analyzed data to produce recommendations for future operations.

Role: Research Assistant

Duration: September - December 2020

The Question

How is the public interacting with and perceiving the K-12 COVID-19 testing process? What may these findings reveal for future operations, such as vaccine distribution?

How is the public interacting with and perceiving the K-12 COVID-19 testing process? What may these findings reveal for future operations, such as vaccine distribution?

The Process

1

Interviews

My team at The Policy Lab ↗ gathered data from opinion metrics, open-ended comments, and one-on-one interviews to address our first question. I was responsble for the last of these data categories.
I was responsble for the last of these data categories. Over a period of two months, my colleague Chris Calley and I conducted 89 interviews. These interviews proved crucial to contextualizing survey responses and, more importantly, to capturing the emotions and lived experience of those engaging with the K-12 COVID-19 testing intiative.

2

Data Analysis

Chris and I conducted all interviews over the phone. We did not record the interviews, but instead transcribed them live.
Using the NVivo software, I uploaded all interview transcripts and singlehandedly coded them by grouping together common responses and marking unusual answers. The coding scheme used allowed for cross tabulation of responses by the week they occurred, the frequency of themes across interviewees, and common trends.

Report Writing

The Solution

1

Offer a broad variety of options for where and when users can get an appointment, as well as who can qualify for one.

2

Send users more information leading up to their appointment, focusing on what to expect and specific instructions on what to do when they arrive.

3

Provide public-facing personnel with frequent updates on procedures coupled with easy to communicate messaging to create better consistency across work streams.

4

Pressure test new processes and systems for preventable issues by introducing a diverse set of users into simulated scenarios.

5

Assign a team member to collect user feedback and provide individualized support.

The Takeaways

This project was one of the most rewarding projects I've had the opportunity to work on. I learned the importance of asking clear and concise interview questions, of actively listening to interviewees without jumping to conclusions, and of conveying people's needs in a manner that benefits and inspires future work.
